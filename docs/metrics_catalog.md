# Metrics Catalog

This catalog is the human-readable business dictionary for the Growth Analytics Platform. MetricFlow YAML is the machine-readable contract; this document explains what each metric means, how it is calculated, why it matters, and how to use it responsibly.

Following the 2026 analytics pattern, governed metric definitions live as version-controlled YAML beside dbt models, while this catalog makes those definitions understandable to analysts, growth managers, and AI agents.

## Active User Metrics

### DAU

- Metric: `dau`
- Business meaning: Number of distinct resolved users active on a given day.
- Calculation: `count_distinct(resolved_user_id)` from `fact_user_events`.
- Grain: Daily by default through `metric_time`.
- Common dimensions: `event__channel`, `event__objective`, `event__country`, `event__device_type`, `event__platform`.
- Business impact: Measures daily product or campaign engagement volume.
- Caveats: This is event-based activity, not app-open-only activity.
- Example question: "What was DAU by channel last week?"

### WAU

- Metric: `wau`
- Business meaning: Number of distinct resolved users active in a week.
- Calculation: Same active-user measure as DAU, queried at weekly time grain.
- Grain: Weekly through `metric_time__week`.
- Common dimensions: Same as DAU.
- Business impact: Smooths daily volatility and helps compare weekly campaign quality.
- Caveats: Currently a weekly-grain query over the same active-user measure, not a rolling 7-day window.
- Example question: "How did WAU trend by objective?"

### MAU

- Metric: `mau`
- Business meaning: Number of distinct resolved users active in a month.
- Calculation: Same active-user measure as DAU, queried at monthly time grain.
- Grain: Monthly through `metric_time__month`.
- Common dimensions: Same as DAU.
- Business impact: High-level growth and retention signal.
- Caveats: Current simulator covers 30 days, so MAU is demo-scale.
- Example question: "What is MAU by acquisition channel?"

## Acquisition And Activation Metrics

### New Signups

- Metric: `new_signups`
- Business meaning: Number of signup events generated by ads or downstream product journeys.
- Calculation: `count_distinct(event_id)` where `event_type = 'signup'`.
- Grain: Daily.
- Common dimensions: `event__channel`, `event__objective`, `event__campaign_id`, `event__country`.
- Business impact: Measures upper-to-mid funnel user acquisition.
- Caveats: Not every TikTok Ads objective has signup as a required step.
- Example question: "Which objective drove the most signups?"

### Activated Users

- Metric: `activated_users`
- Business meaning: Users who reached the app activation milestone.
- Calculation: `count_distinct(resolved_user_id)` where `event_type = 'activation'`.
- Grain: Daily.
- Common dimensions: `event__channel`, `event__objective`, `event__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Separates low-quality signups from users who perform a meaningful action.
- Caveats: Activation currently applies mainly to app-install journeys.
- Example question: "Which channel has the most activated users?"

### Signup To Activation Rate

- Metric: `signup_to_activation_rate`
- Business meaning: Share of signups that become activated users.
- Calculation: `activated_users / new_signups`.
- Grain: Daily or campaign-level.
- Common dimensions: `event__channel`, `event__objective`, `event__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Flags campaign quality beyond raw signup volume.
- Caveats: Use only where activation is meaningful; lead-gen and commerce objectives may not use activation.
- Example question: "What is signup-to-activation rate by channel?"

## Conversion Metrics

### Conversions

- Metric: `conversions`
- Business meaning: Count of objective-specific conversion events.
- Calculation: `count_distinct(conversion_id)` from `fact_conversions`.
- Grain: Daily.
- Common dimensions: `conversion__objective`, `conversion__conversion_type`, `conversion__conversion_channel`, `conversion__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Measures business outcomes across app, commerce, lead, subscription, marketplace, awareness, and offline objectives.
- Caveats: A conversion can mean different things by objective, so always slice or filter by objective when comparing products.
- Example question: "How many conversions did each objective drive?"

### Conversion Value

- Metric: `conversion_value`
- Business meaning: Total canonical value of conversion events.
- Calculation: `sum(conversion_value)` from `fact_conversions`.
- Grain: Daily.
- Common dimensions: Same as conversions.
- Business impact: Turns raw conversion count into monetized business value.
- Caveats: Lead and awareness values are modeled values in this simulator, not booked revenue.
- Example question: "What was conversion value by objective?"

### Purchasing Users

- Metric: `purchasing_users`
- Business meaning: Distinct users with first or repeat purchase activity.
- Calculation: `count_distinct(resolved_user_id)` where event type is `purchase` or `repeat_purchase`.
- Grain: Daily.
- Common dimensions: `event__channel`, `event__objective`, `event__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Useful for commerce-focused campaigns and downstream LTV analysis.
- Caveats: Does not include lead, subscription, marketplace, or offline conversions.
- Example question: "Which channel produced the most purchasing users?"

## Attribution Revenue Metrics

### Attributed Revenue First Touch

- Metric: `attributed_revenue_first_touch`
- Business meaning: Conversion value credited to the earliest eligible touchpoint in the lookback window.
- Calculation: `sum(attributed_revenue)` from `fact_attribution_first_touch`.
- Grain: Current metric date in this portfolio implementation.
- Common dimensions: `attribution_record__channel`, `attribution_record__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Identifies channels that start valuable journeys.
- Caveats: Can over-credit awareness channels and under-credit closing channels.
- Example question: "Which channel creates the most first-touch revenue?"

### Attributed Revenue Last Touch

- Metric: `attributed_revenue_last_touch`
- Business meaning: Conversion value credited to the final eligible touchpoint before conversion.
- Calculation: `sum(attributed_revenue)` from `fact_attribution_last_touch`.
- Common dimensions: `attribution_record_last_touch__channel`, `attribution_record_last_touch__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Useful for understanding conversion-closing channels.
- Caveats: Can over-credit retargeting or bottom-funnel channels.
- Example question: "Which channel closes the most attributed revenue?"

### Attributed Revenue Linear

- Metric: `attributed_revenue_linear`
- Business meaning: Conversion value split equally across all eligible touchpoints.
- Calculation: `sum(attributed_revenue)` from `fact_attribution_linear`.
- Common dimensions: `attribution_record_linear__channel`, `attribution_record_linear__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Gives every touchpoint in the journey equal influence.
- Caveats: Assumes all touches are equally valuable, which is rarely fully true.
- Example question: "How does linear revenue compare by channel?"

### Attributed Revenue Time Decay

- Metric: `attributed_revenue_time_decay`
- Business meaning: Conversion value weighted toward more recent touchpoints with a 7-day half-life.
- Calculation: `sum(attributed_revenue)` from `fact_attribution_time_decay`.
- Common dimensions: `attribution_record_time_decay__channel`, `attribution_record_time_decay__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Balances awareness and closing influence while rewarding recency.
- Caveats: Half-life choice is a business assumption.
- Example question: "What is time-decay attributed revenue by channel?"

### Attributed Revenue Position Based

- Metric: `attributed_revenue_position_based`
- Business meaning: Conversion value credited 40% to first touch, 40% to last touch, and 20% across middle touches.
- Calculation: `sum(attributed_revenue)` from `fact_attribution_position_based`.
- Common dimensions: `attribution_record_position_based__channel`, `attribution_record_position_based__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Useful when both discovery and closing matter.
- Caveats: The 40/20/40 split is a convention, not a causal estimate.
- Example question: "Which channel benefits most under position-based attribution?"

## Attribution Conversion Metrics

The metrics `attributed_conversions_first_touch`, `attributed_conversions_last_touch`, `attributed_conversions_linear`, `attributed_conversions_time_decay`, and `attributed_conversions_position_based` count or fractionally sum conversions credited by each attribution model.

- Business meaning: Number of conversions assigned to channels or campaigns by a selected attribution rule.
- Calculation: first/last use distinct conversion count; fractional models sum `credit_fraction`.
- Business impact: Lets teams calculate CAC and compare channel efficiency by attribution model.
- Caveats: These are attribution outputs, not causal incrementality.
- Example question: "How many conversions does TikTok Ads receive under time-decay attribution?"

## Spend, CAC, And ROAS Metrics

### Total Spend

- Metric: `total_spend`
- Business meaning: Modeled campaign spend.
- Calculation: `sum(daily_spend)` from `fact_campaign_spend_daily`.
- Grain: Daily by campaign, channel, and objective.
- Common dimensions: `campaign_spend_day__channel`, `campaign_spend_day__objective`, `campaign_spend_day__campaign_id`.
- Business impact: Baseline cost input for CAC and ROAS.
- Caveats: Spend is generated from seed assumptions in this portfolio project.
- Example question: "How much did we spend by channel?"

### CAC Metrics

- Metrics: `cac_first_touch`, `cac_last_touch`, `cac_linear`, `cac_time_decay`, `cac_position_based`
- Business meaning: Cost per attributed conversion under each attribution model.
- Calculation: `total_spend / attributed_conversions_<model>`.
- Grain: Metric time.
- Common dimensions: MetricFlow currently exposes these ratio metrics safely by `metric_time`.
- Business impact: Supports budget allocation and channel efficiency comparisons.
- Caveats: Attribution model choice strongly changes CAC interpretation; CAC is not causal lift.
- Example question: "What is CAC under last-touch attribution?"

### ROAS Metrics

- Metrics: `roas_first_touch`, `roas_last_touch`, `roas_linear`, `roas_time_decay`, `roas_position_based`
- Business meaning: Attributed revenue returned per dollar of spend.
- Calculation: `attributed_revenue_<model> / total_spend`.
- Grain: Metric time.
- Business impact: Helps evaluate return on ad spend under different attribution assumptions.
- Caveats: Modeled revenue and seeded spend make this directional, not financial reporting.
- Example question: "Which attribution model gives the highest ROAS?"

## Funnel And Retention Metrics

### Funnel Step Users

- Metric: `funnel_step_users`
- Business meaning: Distinct users who completed each funnel step.
- Calculation: `count_distinct(resolved_user_id)` from `funnel_step_completion`.
- Grain: Daily.
- Common dimensions: `funnel_completion__objective`, `funnel_completion__step_name`, `funnel_completion__first_step_channel`.
- Business impact: Shows where users drop off by objective.
- Caveats: Steps differ by objective; compare within objective before comparing across objectives.
- Example question: "Where do e-commerce users drop off?"

### Retained Users

- Metric: `retained_users`
- Business meaning: Users active in a cohort week after signup.
- Calculation: `sum(retained_users)` from `cohort_retention_matrix`.
- Grain: Signup week and weeks since signup.
- Common dimensions: `cohort_week__weeks_since_signup`, `cohort_week__first_objective`, `cohort_week__acquisition_channel_first_touch`.
- Business impact: Measures post-acquisition quality.
- Caveats: Simulator has only a short time window, so long-term retention is sparse.
- Example question: "How many users returned one week after signup?"

### Cohort Users

- Metric: `cohort_users`
- Business meaning: Signup cohort size at the requested grouping grain.
- Calculation: `sum(cohort_users)` from `cohort_retention_matrix`.
- Grain: Signup week and weeks since signup.
- Business impact: Denominator for retention analysis.
- Caveats: Must be used with cohort dimensions to avoid mixing incompatible cohorts.
- Example question: "How large were weekly signup cohorts?"

### Retention Rate

- Metric: `retention_rate`
- Business meaning: Share of cohort users retained at a given weeks-since-signup offset.
- Calculation: `retained_users / cohort_users`.
- Grain: Signup week and weeks since signup.
- Common dimensions: `cohort_week__weeks_since_signup`, `cohort_week__first_objective`, `cohort_week__acquisition_channel_first_touch`.
- Business impact: Measures whether acquired users continue to engage after signup.
- Caveats: Current retention is weekly, not day-1/day-7 rolling retention.
- Example question: "What is retention by weeks since signup?"
